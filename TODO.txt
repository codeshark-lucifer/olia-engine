** Collision 3D **

Collider: 
Sphere	: Use distance between centers
AABB	: Axis-Aligned Bounding Box
OBB		: Oriented Bounding Box, more math
Capsule	: Combianation of spheres and line
Mesh	: Usually need triangle-based collision

----------------------------------------------------------------
AABB (Axis-Aligned Bounding Box)
- What it is: A box aligned with the world axes (X, Y, Z).
- How it works: Check whether the min/max X, Y, Z values of two boxes overlap.
- Pros:
	> Very fast and simple to compute
	> Great for broad-phase collision detection
- Cons:
	> Not suitable for rotated objects
	> Can be inaccurate for non-box shapes
----------------------------------------------------------------
OBB (Oriented Bounding Box)
- What it is: A 3D box that can be rotated freely.
- How it works: Uses the Separating Axis Theorem (SAT) in 3D to test for overlaps.
- Pros:
	> Much more accurate for rotated objects
	> Better fit than AABB
- Cons:
	> Slower than AABB
	> More complex math and implementation
----------------------------------------------------------------
Sphere Collision
- What it is: A collision shape defined by a center point and a radius.
- How it works: Measure the distance between two sphere centers and compare it to the sum of their radii.
- Pros:
	> Extremely fast
	> Rotation-independent
	> Ideal for characters, particles, and triggers
- Cons:
	> Poor fit for box-like or elongated objects
	> Can cause noticeable false positives
----------------------------------------------------------------
Capsule Collision
- What it is: A cylinder with hemispheres on both ends (commonly used for characters).
- How it works: Check the distance between the central line segments of two capsules (or capsule vs shape).
- Pros:
	> Smooth collision response
	> Excellent for character controllers
	> Handles slopes and steps well
- Cons:
	> More expensive than spheres and AABBs
	> Slightly harder to implement
